  • Rug Glossary
    Traditional

    The world of rugs is wide and varying. Read through our quick guide to navigate the piles and find your perfect style.

    Many traditional rugs feature complex floral patterns that honor designs of the past and imbue a home with a warm Old World spirit.

    Tip: Finding a rug rich with pattern is an easy way to incorporate texture in a space.

previous next
  • Transitional

    Transitional rugs are appreciated for their versatility, favoring abstract displays of color and shape over the more defined patterns in other types of rugs. Because of their organic designs, they're perfect for traditional and contemporary spaces alike.

    Tip: Transitional carpets can help unite rooms filled with an eclectic mix of styles.

previous next
  • Antique

    A carpet can be considered antique only if it is more than 100 years old; semi-antique rugs, which are between 50 and 100 years of age, are equally full of character. When selecting a style, it's best to choose one that speaks to you, regardless of its size or color. Your personal take on patina will always have a place in your home.

    Tip: Pairing the unadorned lines of modern furniture with the intricate details of an antique rug creates a striking visual balance that allows all elements to shine.

previous next
  • Overdyed

    Overdyed rugs generally start with traditional patterns but are reinvigorated with an unexpected, saturated wash of color, adding a contemporary edge to the composition.

    Tip: For a welcome touch of drama, keep the furniture sparse. Rugs that are wild with pattern or bold in color can often serve as the centerpiece of a room.

previous next
  • Flat-Weave

    Found all over the world, flat-weave rugs are often noted for their graphic patterns and low profile. While this rug's type of weave is centuries old, it can feel surprisingly modern.

    Tip: Because of their durable construction, flat-weave carpets are an excellent choice for high-traffic areas.
